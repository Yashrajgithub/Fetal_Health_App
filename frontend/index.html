Understanding Fetal Distress Detection

🏥

What is Fetal Distress?

Fetal distress refers to signs before or during childbirth indicating that the fetus is not well, often due to oxygen deprivation or other complications. Early detection is crucial for preventing adverse outcomes and ensuring maternal and fetal safety.

📊

Why CTG Reports Matter

Cardiotocography (CTG) measures the fetal heart rate (FHR) and uterine contractions, helping doctors monitor fetal well-being throughout pregnancy and labor. CTG provides continuous, non-invasive monitoring that can detect early signs of distress.

🤖

How AI Improves Diagnosis

Machine learning models such as the XGBoost Classifier analyze 21 quantitative CTG parameters to classify fetal states into:

  • 🟢 Normal - Healthy fetal status
  • 🟠 Suspect - Requires close monitoring
  • 🔴 Pathologic - Immediate attention needed
⚡

Key Benefits

Our AI-powered system provides objective analysis, reduces human error in interpretation, enables early detection of complications, and supports clinical decision-making with data-driven insights. This technology empowers healthcare providers to make faster, more accurate assessments.

Our model has been trained on extensive CTG datasets and validated against clinical outcomes. The XGBoost algorithm excels at handling complex, non-linear relationships in medical data, achieving high accuracy in fetal state classification.
